Hi! I’m John.

I’m a recent graduate of Cornell University and a news assistant at The New York Times.

When I moved to upstate New York from South Korea in middle school, I struggled adapt to life in English, my second language. Joining my high school newspaper helped me to overcome the fear that I couldn’t ever speak or write like a native speaker.

That was also when I realized I wanted to become a journalist. I continued to do reporting in Ithaca at The Ithaca Journal and The Cornell Daily Sun. I also developed an interest in investigative reporting and audio production throughout college, working as a news producer and host at a community radio station in Ithaca and at the student podcast State of the Pod.

I earned a B.A. in the College Scholar Program, where I designed my own interdisciplinary major and completed a thesis in intellectual history. This field has taught me to be a careful reader of my sources — a lesson that has shaped how I tell my stories as a journalist.

On my free time I try to spend as much time as I can outdoors (hiking, running, or swimming in a gorge) and/or with my family of seven.

Please take a look at my portfolio and feel free to reach out to me!
